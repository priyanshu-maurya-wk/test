The Obsidian Noir I is the founding piece of our most iconic series — a vessel that captures the primal energy of volcanic formation. The glass itself is formed from basalt harvested on the slopes of Mount Etna in Sicily, transformed under 1,200°C into a substance both ancient and entirely new.

The silhouette was designed by maison director Isabelle Fontaine-Noir after three years of sketching. The final form — a slight inward curve at the shoulder that swells outward again before the base — was inspired by the movement of lava under pressure.

The platinum collar is hand-soldered by our Parisian metalsmith workshop in the 11th arrondissement. Each collar bears the master's mark. The hand-etched midnight pattern — 180 individual incisions per vessel — is cut over three days by engraver Zuzana Horáčková.

The Obsidian Noir I requires minimal but attentive care to preserve its character across generations.

  • Never place in a dishwasher. The thermal shock will cause irreparable stress fractures.
  • Clean the glass body with a soft, lint-free cloth dampened with cool water and a drop of mild, unscented dish soap.
  • Clean the platinum collar with a dry microfibre cloth only. Do not use metal polishes — the platinum plating is delicate.
  • Store upright. Avoid placing on uneven surfaces that could cause rocking and impact.
  • Keep away from prolonged direct sunlight; the basalt glass can heat unevenly and cause internal stress.
  • A full care guide on archival paper is enclosed with your acquisition.
