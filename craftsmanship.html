The Process

From Molten Glass
to Masterwork

01
Material Selection

The Hunt for Exceptional Glass

Our sourcing team travels twice yearly to Bohemia, Murano, and specialist suppliers in Kyushu, Japan to handpick raw materials. Each batch of silica sand, lead-free cullet, or mineral colourant is tested in our laboratory for purity, refractive index, and thermal stability before a single gram enters our ateliers.

02
The Gather

Molten Glass, Living Breath

At 1,200°C, our master glassblowers — maestri soffiatori — gather molten glass onto the blowpipe with a single, assured movement. The temperature must be read in the colour of the glow: amber for gathering, bright orange for shaping, cooling yellow for finishing. Years of practice condense into each silent moment at the furnace.

03
Shaping

Three Days, One Form

Unlike mass production, each Noir Vessels piece is shaped over two to three days in iterative sessions — reheating, blowing, stretching, and pressing — until the silhouette matches the original wax maquette exactly. The maestro works from memory and intuition, with the maquette present only as a final reference, never a crutch.

04
Annealing

The Art of Controlled Cooling

After forming, each piece enters the lehr — a carefully calibrated annealing oven — where it is cooled from 540°C to room temperature over 18 hours. This slow release of thermal stress is the invisible difference between a vessel that lasts a lifetime and one that will shatter under the slightest change.

05
Decoration

Forty Hands of Finishing

Depending on series, the cooled vessel passes through engravers, gilders, metal cagers, lacquerers, and polishers. Our Gold Meridian pieces require 22 separate finishing stations before reaching the quality control chamber. Every surface is touched by human hands — no automated buffing, no robotic spraying.

06
Authentication

The Signature and the Seal

Every finished vessel is examined under UV and raking light for 90 minutes. It receives a hand-engraved edition number and the master's personal mark on its base. A wax-sealed provenance document — with the names of each artisan who touched the piece — accompanies every acquisition.

The Makers

Meet Our
Artisans

Behind every vessel, a human story. A dedication measured not in years but in decades.

Luigi Venier

Maestro Soffiatore · Murano

Third-generation glassblower from a Murano dynasty stretching back to 1887. Luigi has shaped over 4,000 Noir Vessels pieces in his 38-year career. He describes the ideal gather as "breathing in, then giving the glass your intention."

Zuzana Horáčková

Master Engraver · Bohemia

Zuzana trained at the Škola Uměleckého Řemesla in Železný Brod for seven years before joining our atelier in 2011. Her copper-wheel engravings are considered among the finest in Europe — each motif requires three days of concentrated work.

Marco Ferrara

Master Gilder · Florence

Trained under the late Giacomo Lippi of the Opificio delle Pietre Dure, Marco applies gold leaf with an ox-hair brush so fine it barely disturbs the air. His work on the Gold Meridian series has been described by critics as "the standard by which all gilding is judged."

Hélène Marchand

Silversmith · Paris

A graduate of the École Boulle, Hélène crafts the sterling silver cages that define the Amethyst Heritage series. Each cage is forged from a single sheet of sterling silver, bent and soldered in our 16th arrondissement atelier over the course of four days.

Our History

2008

Noir Vessels founded in Paris by Isabelle Fontaine-Noir. First atelier opens in the 16th arrondissement.

2011

Partnership with Fornace Venier in Murano. Launch of Crystal Reserve series. First sell-out in under 48 hours.

2014

Gold Meridian debuts at TEFAF Maastricht. Acquisition by the Victoria & Albert Museum collection.

2017

Atelier Bohemia opens in Železný Brod. Amethyst Heritage series launched. Waitlist exceeds 600 names.

2020

Bespoke commission programme launches. Tokyo atelier opens. Obsidian Series wins the Grand Prix de la Création.

2024

Emerald Vault series debuts. Collector community reaches 12,000 worldwide. Online boutique relaunched.
